Mission

To elevate mental health awareness, reduce stigma, and expand culturally responsive support for Latino individuals who have undergone chemotherapy—through bilingual education, peer connection, and trusted community partnerships—so they and their families can heal and thrive

Vision

A future where Latino cancer survivors and their families have equitable access to trauma‑informed, culturally grounded mental health care; feel seen and supported in their language and culture; and experience resilient, dignified survivorship within strong, connected communities..

Community Outreach

At Humanity Touches, we mobilize the community to provide for unhoused members, support seniors, strengthen food security, provide inclusive teaching for people with disabilities, and offer free pickleball clinics for underserved families—rooted in bilingual, culturally responsive, and trauma‑informed practices. Our outreach complements our core focus on mental health awareness for Latino cancer survivors by meeting families where they are with practical resources, movement, connection, and dignity.

What We Do

Caring for Homeless Neighbors

  • Street outreach with care kits (hygiene, socks, snacks, resource cards)
  • Donations with lunch sacks (sandwiches, protein bars, beverages, chips)
  • Meal trays for friendship shelters
  • Bagels & doughnuts donated through bakery partners

Supporting Seniors

  • Wellness check-ins by phone
  • Social circles: gentle movement, music, memory-friendly activities
  • Navigation for utility assistance & transportation

Food Drive & Food Access

  • Monthly food drives with culturally familiar groceries & produce
  • Deliveries to homebound seniors & chemo survivors
  • Partnerships with food banks, grocers & farms

Inclusive Teaching for People with Disabilities

  • Reading & writing skill-building classes in English

Pickleball for All (Free Clinics)

  • Beginner-friendly clinics for youth & families
  • Gear provided — paddles, balls & nets
  • Adaptive sessions for individuals with disabilities
  • Movement, joy & social connection to support mental health
  • Partnerships with parks, schools & community centers

FAQs

Who can participate?
Anyone in our service area; priority for seniors, unhoused neighbors, people with disabilities, and cancer survivors.
Is it free?
Yes—services and clinics are provided at no cost.
Do you offer mental health resources?
Yes—education, support groups, and referrals to counseling and psycho-oncology.
How do I refer someone?
Use the referral form or call [Phone Number]
Do I need pickleball gear or experience?
No—gear is provided and coaches will teach basics; adaptive options available

Local parks and community centers; see [Link to calendar] for addresses and times
